Definitions used in this document

  • The 'Supplier' is the legal entity that will enter into the contract unless it is a collaboration of multiple legal entities, in which case it is the name of the collaboration (for the avoidance of doubt GP practices should enter the name of the PCN Grouping)
  • Where the supplier is a collaboration of Supplier Parties, for example a PCN Grouping, please insert the name of the legal entities that make up the PCN Grouping, for the avoidance of doubt, this will be the practice names.

  • Healthcare premises:
    • A place to which people are admitted for the purpose of receiving a regulated activity to meet healthcare needs (for example a hospital or day surgery unit)
    • A place in which people live as their main or sole place of residence or in which they are educated, and they receive care or treatment there (for example, a care home)
    • An urgent care facility (for example, a walk-in centre)
    • The premises where a primary care provider carries on regulated activity (for example a GP, out-of-hours, dental, community substance misuse service or sexual assault referral centre)
    • The premises from which a registered provider organises or manages care that is delivered to people in their homes (for example a domiciliary care (home care), supported living or shared lives service)
    • A place from where an ambulance or patient transport service is managed
    • A stand-alone permanent diagnostic or screening facility
    • A place from where urgent remote clinical advice and triage is managed (for example, an NHS 111 service)
  • Non-healthcare premises:
    • A place identified as a location when regulated activities are being delivered at or managed from places that would not constitute a location under definition of a healthcare premises (for example, mobile medical facilities, online GP services, community health services)
