Last updated: 10-Jun-20
The NHS Business Services Authority (the “Authority”) and the supplier of your pharmacy system (the “Supplier” and the “System”) makes the RTEC (real time exemption checking) service (the “Service") available to you (the “Pharmacy”) on the following conditions.

1. The Pharmacy must use the Service for the sole purpose of genuine (not speculative) checks on patient entitlement to prescription exemptions.

2. The Pharmacy will send the Authority the patient's date of birth, postcode, NHS number, forename and surname (“Pharmacy Data”) via the System's API connection to the Authority's RTEC system (the “Exchange”).

3. The Pharmacy must use the “Y” or “Unknown” response it receives from the Service (“Response Data”) for the sole purpose of ascertaining the patient's entitlement in real time.

4. The Pharmacy must not use the Response Data for an incompatible purpose or disclose the Response Data further unless the law requires or permits the Pharmacy to do so.

5. The Pharmacy must keep the Response Data confidential and secure. The Pharmacy must only share the Response Data with the patient unless the law requires or permits the Pharmacy to share the Response Data with a third party.

6. The Pharmacy must not transfer the Response Data outside the UK.

7. The Pharmacy must keep the System and its access to the Exchange secure from unauthorised access.

8. The Pharmacy acknowledges that it is possible that it and/or the Authority also process other personal data in connection with the Service, for example in relation to requests, complaints or other issues (“Other Data”).

9. If the Authority becomes aware of a personal data breach or security incident in relation to the Pharmacy Data, Response Data, Other Data, Service or the System then it may shut down the Exchange without notice to the Pharmacy pending an investigation; if this happens, the Pharmacy should contact the Supplier for updates on restoration of the Service.

10. If the Authority receives a data subject request or other communication that relates to the Pharmacy, the Authority will pass it to the Supplier to pass on to the Pharmacy. The Pharmacy should do the same if it receives communications for the Authority.

11. The Authority and the Pharmacy are each independent controllers under data protection legislation. Each of them must ensure that its respective processing of the Pharmacy Data, Response Data and any Other Data (together, "Relevant Data") complies with all applicable data protection and privacy legislation and the Information Commissioner's data sharing code of practice. The Authority is not responsible for the Pharmacy's data protection compliance and the Pharmacy is not responsible for the Authority's data protection compliance. The Authority's NHS Prescription Services privacy notice at describes its processing. The Authority will supply the Pharmacy with a notice to display in its premises. The Pharmacy is responsible for providing its own privacy notice to patients. The Authority's legal basis for processing is the public task (Article 6(1)(e) GDPR), as evidenced in regulation 18A of the National Health Service (Charges for Drugs and Appliances) Regulations 2015. The Authority's condition for processing health data is the management of healthcare systems in Article 9(2)(i) GDPR (with the safeguards in Article 9(3)) and schedule 1 Part 1 paragraph 2 of the Data Protection Act 2018 (with the safeguards in section 11(1) of that Act). The Pharmacy is responsible for determining its own legal bases and conditions for processing Relevant Data. The Authority and the Pharmacy are each responsible for determining their own retention periods for categories of Relevant Data.

12. The Authority may revise these conditions from time to time. The Supplier will communicate any revisions to the Pharmacy. The Pharmacy must accept the revisions in order to continue to use the Service.

13. The Pharmacy may stop using the Service at any time by adjusting the settings in the System.
