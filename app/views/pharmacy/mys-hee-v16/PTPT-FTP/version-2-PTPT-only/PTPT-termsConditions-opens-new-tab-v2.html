Adaption of the Main Body Terms:

  1. There shall be no signed copy of the NHS Education Agreement.
  2. Your acceptance of the terms and conditions on behalf of the Provider will be acknowledged through your completing the details and tick box below.
  3. NHS England’s acceptance of your offer will be through the email to you confirming the acceptance of the training of the trainee(s). As such, the Main Body Terms are varied as follows:
    • 3.1 The definition of “Commencement Date” shall be replaced: “means the date of the email from NHS England confirming the acceptance of the Provider’s offer to take the PTPT(s)(as defined in Schedule 1)”.
    • 3.2 The “Provider” or person who is contracting with NHS England will be the corporate body, individual, or partnership that is associated with the Organisation Data Service (ODS) code from which the application for the PTPT(s) (as defined in Schedule 1) is made.

SCHEDULE 1: Provider Services

  1. Milestones: There are no separate Milestones associated with these services.
  2. Manager: The individual completing this form on behalf of the Provider shall be the Manager, unless or until the Provider gives notice of an alternative Manager.
  3. Service specification:
    • 3.1 The activity covered within this Schedule 1 is non-recurrent for the period described below and within Schedule 2 of this agreement relates to the education and training of pre-registration trainee pharmacy technicians (PTPT).
    • 3.2 Activity will be for a maximum twenty-four-month period, unless suspension, necessary delay or cessation shall at any time apply.
    • 3.3 The Provider will accommodate a PTPT for both Year 1 and Year 2 of training, with funding provided as per Schedule 2 of this agreement.
    • 3.4 The Provider is responsible for employing PTPT(s) in line with their existing policies and procedures.
    • 3.5 The Provider as employer will ensure appropriate DBS and reference checks are carried out as per the employing organisations HR policies and confirm that the PTPT is 16 years or older.
    • 3.6 The Provider as employer will ensure the PTPT meets apprenticeship funding rules before appointment. Funding rules are: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/apprenticeship-funding-rules
    • 3.7 Funding has been allocated on the basis that PTPTs will be employed as per the national minimum wage rate.
  4. Programme Requirements:
    • PTPT Training Programmes will:
    • 4.1 Meet the requirements of the GPhC 2017 IET standards for pharmacy technicians.
    • 4.2 Be overseen by a designated Educational Supervisor, who must be a GPhC registered pharmacy professional.
    • 4.3 Have a work-based Practice / Clinical Supervisor throughout each care setting.
    • 4.4 Include training and development to ensure the final accuracy check of items dispensed by others and training in medicines reconciliation.
    • 4.5 Provide experiential learning opportunities.
    • 4.6 Demonstrate quality benchmarking aligned to the NHS England Education Quality Framework.
    • 4.7 Have a learning plan (demonstrably) in place before the PTPT starts.
    • 4.8 Have placement objectives for each care setting covering the full 24-month training programme.
    • 4.9 Where the training programme includes placements, the Provider must ensure that the PTPT is fully inducted into each placement and appropriate supervision is in place.
  5. Education Provision:
    • 5.1 Education provision will be through the Level 3 Pharmacy Technician (Integrated) apprenticeship standard.
    • 5.2 The Provider will procure the apprenticeship provision which shall be a GPhC-approved national qualification or course by a GPhC accredited training provider.
    • 5.3 The Provider is responsible for ensuring that the PTPT meets the apprenticeship providers’ entry requirements and undertakes any pre-screening as required.
    • 5.4 The PTPT must be given a minimum of one day per week to attend their apprenticeship provider and / or complete any distance/e-learning. This should be part of their paid employment and may contribute towards their ‘off-the-job' training requirement.
  6. Designated Educational Supervisor:
    • 6.1 The Provider must have a named registered pharmacy professional to act as a designated Educational Supervisor who has been registered with the GPhC for at least two years at the point when the PTPT commences their Programme.
    • 6.2 A designated Educational Supervisor is someone who is selected, appropriately trained and responsible for the overall Supervision and management of the PTPT’s educational progress during the 24-month training Programme.
    • 6.3 To become a designated Educational Supervisor, candidates must: be a registered pharmacy professional, have been practising as a registered pharmacy professional for at least two years in pharmacy, meet the GPhC guidance on tutoring and supervising pharmacy professionals in training and meet the apprenticeship Provider’s requirement.
    • 6.4 The designated Educational Supervisor should work collaboratively with the Practice / Clinical Supervisors to ensure the overall training programme meets the GPhC 2017 IET standards for pharmacy technicians and monitor the PTPTs progress throughout the training programme in order to give feedback on progression and at the annual appraisal.
    • 6.5 It is expected that the Educational Supervisor will also meet their PTPT at least once a month to provide holistic care, review progress and support.
  7. Practice/Named Clinical Supervisors
    • 7.1 A Practice / Named Clinical Supervisor in pharmacy is someone who is selected, appropriately trained and responsible for overseeing a specified PTPT’s work and provide developmental feedback during a period of training. This role requires appropriate assessment skills.
    • 7.2 Practice / Named Clinical Supervisors will support PTPTs to identify opportunities for learning in the workplace and provide supervision of PTPTs on a day-to-day basis, identifying PTPTs requiring additional support.
    • 7.3 Practice / Named Clinical Supervisors will be involved in and contribute to a work-based learning culture.
    • 7.4 The Practice / Named Clinical Supervisor should meet with the PTPT at the beginning of a 12-week period to agree learning outcomes, training and support. Following this, Practice / Named Clinical Supervisors and PTPTs should meet mid-12-week period to provide interim feedback and support and then again at the end of the 12-week period to give summative feedback.

SCHEDULE 2: FUNDING

  1. Funding:
    • 1.1 Providers will be paid monthly, 2 months in arrears, by twelve monthly (pro-rata) payments which will total £15,053, in year 1 (one) of the two-year training programme.
    • 1.2 Providers will be paid monthly, 2 months in arrears, by twelve monthly (pro-rata) payments which will total £15,053, in Year 2 (two) of the two-year training programme.
    • 1.3 All funding offers within this Schedule 2 are contingent upon NHS England receiving the necessary funding approvals for the relevant Financial Year.
    • 1.4 NHS England reserves the right to withdraw from or suspend any activity, where finance is either delayed, suspended or ceased. In such cases, NHS England will follow the Termination process under clause 37 of the main body terms .
    • 1.5 NHS England will provide funding contingent upon the Provider delivering Year 1 and Year 2 PTPT training to the PTPT confirmed as accepted by NHS England.
    • 1.6 Repayments of education and training contributions due to withdrawals If a PTPT withdraws from training the Provider must inform NHS England immediately. The monthly payments will stop from the month the PTPT withdraws. If additional payments are made, the Provider will be liable to return the additional money.
